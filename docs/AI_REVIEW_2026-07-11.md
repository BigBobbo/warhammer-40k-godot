# AI Review — 2026-07-11

A ground-up review of the game AI: how it decides, how holistic those
decisions are, and how its thinking is presented to the player. Evidence
comes from a full live AI-vs-AI game of the default matchup — **Recon
Stomps (Orks, P1) vs Custodes Lions (Adeptus Custodes, P2), both AI at
Hard** — played in the real windowed UI, plus a read-through of the
decision pipeline (`AIDecisionMaker.gd`, ~19.7k lines, `AIPlayer.gd`,
`GameEventLog.gd`, `GameLogPanel.gd`).

## 1. How the AI works today (architecture summary)

The AI is a **utility-scoring agent with partial army-level planning**:

- `AIPlayer` (autoload) orchestrates: watches phases, asks
  `AIDecisionMaker.decide()` for ONE action at a time, submits it through
  the normal action pipeline, repeats until the phase ends.
- `AIDecisionMaker.decide()` routes to a per-phase solver. The movement
  solver was the most developed: objective evaluation (state, OC, VP
  stakes from the actual mission cards, round urgency, tempo) → greedy
  unit-to-objective assignment (OC-need bookkeeping, incoming-OC intents,
  screening/corridor-blocking passes) → per-unit execution with many
  tactical refinements (threat maps, weapon-range preservation, heavy
  weapon holds, half-range closing, lone-operative safety, …).
- Reasoning transparency was already a first-class feature: thinking
  steps and structured decision records accumulate per decision and reach
  the game log as collapsible "AI thinking" cards, some with board-linked
  hover arrows (chosen green / rejected red).

This is a solid foundation — comparable in spirit to XCOM's utility
scoring (position/action scores from named factors) and to the
task-allocation architecture used in TBS research (score all
(unit, task) pairs, assign greedily).

## 2. What the live game showed (the problems)

Final score of the review game: **Orks 67 — Custodes 28** (primary VP
45 vs 12). Both armies ended with most units alive — the game was decided
by objective play, which is exactly where the problems live.

### 2.1 The plan was recomputed before every action — so it wasn't a plan

The army-wide assignment was re-solved from scratch before *every unit's
action*. The log announced one battle plan, then executed a different
one, because each single-unit decision re-shuffled everything:

- Plan: `Stompa: MOVE obj_center` → executed: `Stompa moves toward obj_nml_2`
- Plan: `Warbikers ×2: MOVE obj_home_2` → executed: one to `obj_nml_2`, one to `obj_center`
- Plan: `Stormboyz: SCREEN` → executed: `moves toward obj_nml_2`

**9 of 10 movement plans contained duplicate objective assignments** (up
to 8 units on `obj_center` in round 5) — partly deliberate massing the
log never justified, partly the fallback pass assigning every leftover
unit independently with no shared state. This is the "multiple units
towards the same objective" behavior the review was asked about.

### 2.2 The log actively misled about WHY

Of 95 per-unit movement decision cards in one game:

- **55 (58%)** claimed the AI "chose" an option scoring LOWER than one it
  "rejected", with no explanation (the coordination override was real,
  the narration just didn't say so).
- **45 (47%)** named a different objective than the action header —
  the card's "chosen" was re-derived from the destination's nearest
  objective instead of the actual assignment. Example:
  header "Custodian Guard moves toward obj_nml_1" with a card reading
  "chose Move obj_home_2 (score −1.5), ✗ rejected obj_center (18.2)".

### 2.3 Melee hunts silently overrode the plan

Melee seekers decided to abandon their objective at *execution time*, so
the plan said "MOVE obj_3" while the unit actually chased an enemy — and
the objective's OC-need stayed "covered" in the planner's books while
nobody was going there.

### 2.4 Strategic Reserves lost the Custodes the game

The Custodes AI put **950 pts (47.5%) into Strategic Reserves** (legal
under the engine's 50% cap, ruinous in practice): 7 units — effectively
5 maneuver elements at 6" move — had to cover 5 objectives for two
rounds. Primary VP 12 vs 45.

### 2.5 Small display bugs

The phase banner and the AI Actions overlay always displayed "Round 1 /
Rd 1" — they read a nonexistent `meta.round` key (`battle_round` is the
real one).

### 2.6 Two crash-class bugs found while validating (both pre-existing)

- **Shooting UI stack overflow (frozen game):** when a weapon's only
  UI-eligible target is rejected by the phase (LoS disagreement), the
  reject handler resyncs the controller, the resync refreshes the weapon
  tree, the tree auto-assigns the single target again, the phase rejects
  again — a synchronous cycle that overflowed the stack and wedged the
  main loop (observed live: the process spewed 2 GB of backtraces).
  Fixed with an `_in_resync` guard: during a resync the tree renders but
  does not re-emit assignments.
- **Roll-off 200-action burn:** `CHOOSE_DEPLOYMENT` /
  `CONFIRM_FIRST_TURN` are offered TO the roll-off winner, but AIPlayer
  stamps the *evaluating* player onto every decision — when the winner
  isn't the active player, the submission is rejected in a loop until
  the action-cap escape fires (~200 wasted actions, minutes of wall
  time, log spam). Fixed with `_ai_player_override`: roll-off decisions
  carry the offered player through the stamp.

## 3. What other games do (references used)

- **Into the Breach** — total intent transparency: enemies broadcast the
  exact action they will take; the designers call the game "a puzzle game
  wrapped in a strategy game", which only works because intent is shown
  ([GDC 2019 postmortem](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1025772/-Into-the-Breach-Design)).
  Lesson applied: the AI should *commit* to a plan and show it; the log
  is the AI's "broadcast".
- **XCOM (GDC 2013)** — per-position utility from named factors
  (distance, flanking, cover, proximity penalties), ~17 behavior sets on
  top ([PC Gamer's GDC 2013 write-up](https://www.pcgamer.com/gdc-2013-the-ai-tricks-behind-xcom-assassins-creed-3-and-warframe/)).
  The codebase already does this well; the gap was coordination, not
  scoring.
- **TBS task-allocation research** (Bergsma & Spronck; AIPG07 plan
  adaptation) — generate (asset, task) bids, allocate globally,
  *adapt the plan only on failure* rather than re-solving continuously
  ([Adaptive Intelligence for Turn-based Strategy Games](https://www.spronck.net/pubs/BNAIC2008Bergsma.pdf),
  [Game AI for a Turn-based Strategy Game with Plan Adaptation](https://www.cse.lehigh.edu/~munoz/Publications/AIPG07.pdf),
  [Designing AI Algorithms For Turn-Based Strategy Games](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/designing-ai-algorithms-for-turn-based-strategy-games)).
  Lesson applied directly: plan persistence with consume/replan semantics.

## 4. Changes made in this pass

All in `AIDecisionMaker.gd` unless noted; every tag below appears in code
comments at the change sites.

1. **COORD-4 — persistent battle plan.** The movement assignment is
   computed once per movement phase, stored per player, and consumed as
   units act. Re-plans happen only for cause (a unit not in the plan
   becomes movable — e.g. disembark; a consumed unit is re-offered —
   e.g. failed move) and are narrated ("Re-planning movement: …"). The
   announced plan is now the executed plan; a new log gate
   (`GameEventLog.movement_plan_integrity()`) enforces it in a windowed
   scenario.
2. **COORD-4 — attack intents in the plan.** The melee chase-vs-objective
   gates (previously three divergent inline copies) are consolidated into
   `_melee_chase_gate()` and run at *planning* time. Hunts appear in the
   plan as `ATTACK <enemy>` entries, and the abandoned objective's OC
   need re-opens so the backfill pass can send another unit.
3. **COORD-4 — support spreading.** The leftover-unit fallback now shares
   a per-objective commitment ledger and applies diminishing returns
   (`SUPPORT_STACK_PENALTY`), so spare units fan out instead of stacking.
4. **COORD-5 — projected contest pressure.** Objective evaluation adds
   `projected_enemy_oc` (enemy OC within one move + 2" of the marker),
   and the assignment sizes OC needs against it (capped by
   `PROJECTED_NEED_CAP`). Deliberate reinforcement is narrated
   ("capturing it — enemy can bring ~4 OC next move").
5. **Narration integrity.** Decision cards are built from the actual
   assignment (chosen = assigned objective, with the enriched reason);
   attack/screen assignments get a synthesized chosen entry; and whenever
   the chosen option scores below a rejected one the card appends
   "↳ why not the top score: …" with the real coordination reason.
6. **COORD-6 — reserves board-presence guard.** Reserve declarations pay
   a growing penalty as Strategic Reserves pass 25% of army points and as
   on-board units approach the objective count. The Custodes list now
   keeps ~500 pts in reserve instead of 950. The cap decision is narrated.
7. **Display fixes** (`Main.gd`): phase banner and AI overlay now show
   the real battle round.
8. **Headless-test unblocking:** `AIDecisionMaker` referenced the
   `DebugLogger` autoload directly (17 sites), which fails to compile in
   `--script` mode and silently broke several AI test suites; replaced
   with a late-bound helper matching the file's own convention. Also
   repaired the stale `test_ai_multi_phase_planning.gd` (pre-per-player
   plan shapes) — 34/34 pass again.

New tests: `tests/test_ai_battle_plan.gd` (15 asserts: persistence,
consume/replan, spreading, projected pressure, attack conversion) and the
windowed scenario `tests/scenarios/sp/ai_battle_plan_log_integrity.json`.

## 5. Validation

- Headless: new suite 15/15; coordination suite 7/7; movement decisions
  33 passed / 2 failed — the 2 failures are pre-existing on the base
  commit (verified by running base + only the DebugLogger fix); charge
  61-62/0; focus fire 41/0; multi-phase planning 34/0 (was: file didn't
  parse); fall back 4/4; roll-off 20/0.
- Windowed: `ai_battle_plan_log_integrity` scenario **13 passed / 0
  failed** (plan block in the log, ≥3 executed actions checked against
  the announced plan, zero mismatches, decision cards rendered).
- Live full AI-vs-AI re-run, same lists (Recon Stomps vs Custodes Lions,
  both Hard), 5 complete rounds, `verify_delivery` PASS, zero debug-log
  errors. Measured from the new game's full log dump vs the pre-change
  game:

| Metric | Before (0.19) | After (0.20) |
|---|---|---|
| Decision cards | 95 | 73 |
| "Chose worse" without explanation | 55 (58%) | **0** (50 overrides, all explained) |
| Header vs card objective contradiction | 45 (47%) | **0** |
| Announced plan vs executed moves | routine divergence | **0 mismatches** over 89 actions / 10 plans |
| Unnecessary re-plans | (plan re-solved every action) | **0** |
| Announced ATTACK moves executed | n/a (silent overrides) | 16 |
| Custodes Strategic Reserves | 950 pts (47.5%) | 500 pts (25%) |
| Result | Orks 67 — Custodes 28 | Custodes 66 — Orks 34 |

  Two games with different dice are directional, not statistical — but
  the Custodes flipping from a 12-primary-VP shutout loss to a
  39-primary-VP win in the same matchup, with the improved board play
  visible on screen (three objectives held at the end), is the intended
  direction. The full 2×10-seed soak remains the quantitative gate
  (next steps).

## 6. Recommended next steps (not in this pass)

- **Cross-phase plan continuity:** shooting already suppresses charge
  targets and movement biases toward charge lanes; a per-turn "focus"
  summary card (movement plan + focus-fire plan + charge plan in one
  block) would let the player read the whole turn's intent at once.
- **Matchup learning for reserves:** the 25% comfort line is static; a
  smarter rule would weigh enemy alpha-strike threat vs board coverage.
- **Aircraft movement:** the Wazbom Blastajet still burns its activation
  on rejected normal moves every round ("AIRCRAFT can only make ingress
  moves") — needs aircraft-aware movement decisions (known residual from
  the 2026-07-10 mechanism-fix report).
- **Deployment role classifier:** Stormboyz/Warbikers deploy as "durable
  ranged unit — positioning for firing lanes" (they carry token pistols);
  melee-focused fast units should classify as assault elements and deploy
  for movement lanes instead. The narration is honest about what the AI
  thinks — the thinking itself is what needs the fix.
- **Benchmark rebaseline:** run the 2×10-seed soak
  (`tests/run_ai_benchmark.sh`) and commit a new
  `tests/bench_baselines/` report so the coordination changes get a
  quantified before/after (the fixture armies differ from the lists used
  here).
