Method. Every issue is worked in dependency order from ISSUES.md, lands with its own regression tests (the suite grew from 562 → 759 checks), is verified against windowed scenarios over the in-game MCP bridge where player-facing, and is committed individually. The 63-entry golden-master corpus pins the exact dice and state-diffs of both attack-resolution paths, so every refactor is proven byte-identical. The edition switch (GameConstants.edition, default 10) lets 11th-edition behavior land incrementally and provably without breaking the playable game.

Bugs found & fixed along the way

Fight-phase engagement ring rendered at 0.635" instead of 1" — millimeters passed to a pixel API (found during ISS-002, fixed).
AI scored impossible probabilities: skill ≤1 as P=1.0 (true cap 5/6 — a natural 1 always misses) and skill ≥7 as P=0.0 (true floor 1/6 — a natural 6 always hits). Overwatch evaluation was the worst affected (fixed in ISS-014).
Silent multiplayer divergence: only advance rolls were seed-synced; a dropped result RPC desynced peers undetectably (fixed in ISS-015 with per-action seeds + state-hash verification).
Typo'd weapon abilities silently never fired — regex parsing ignored unknown tokens; now a load error (ISS-003).
Documented latent issues: delegated actions double-apply their diffs (idempotent for sets, would double-append adds); formations actions bypass undo entirely; melee resolution returns success:true with empty dice on invalid assignments; a model at exactly (0,0) is treated as unpositioned. All recorded in the tracker against their owning issues.
